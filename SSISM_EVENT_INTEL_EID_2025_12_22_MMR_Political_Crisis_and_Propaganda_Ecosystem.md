# Executive Summary

This dossier consolidates **three converging dimensions** of Myanmar’s ongoing crisis as of **December 2025**:

1. **International escalation** — United Nations Security Council (UNSC) convenes a closed-door session on Myanmar amid worsening conditions.
2. **Judicial accountability pathways** — Formalized legal cooperation between France and the UN’s Independent Investigative Mechanism for Myanmar (IIMM).
3. **Domestic militarization & information warfare** — Junta election narratives, air-power escalation, and the systematic deployment of online propaganda actors (“Military Lobbyists” / “6800-Kyat Accounts”).

The analysis situates these events within a **broader authoritarian continuity architecture**, combining kinetic force, legal impunity, and digital narrative control.

# 1. International Diplomatic Signal: UNSC Closed Session on Myanmar

## 1.1 Event Overview

The **United Nations Security Council** announced a **dedicated closed-door meeting on Myanmar**, scheduled for **22 December 2025**, responding to the rapidly deteriorating humanitarian, political, and security environment.

### Key Briefers
- **Julie Bishop** — UN Special Envoy on Myanmar  
- **Tan Sri Othman Hashim** — ASEAN Special Envoy (Malaysia, 2025 ASEAN Chair)

The meeting signals **elevated international concern**, though without guarantees of enforcement or binding resolutions.

## 1.2 Analytical Assessment

| Indicator | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Diplomatic urgency | High |
| Enforcement likelihood | Low–Moderate |
| Symbolic pressure | High |
| Impact on junta behavior | Historically limited |

The UNSC session functions primarily as a **normative and reputational pressure mechanism**, rather than an immediate coercive one.

# 2. Accountability Vector: France–IIMM Judicial Cooperation

## 2.1 Legal Development

On **17 December 2025**, the **French National Assembly unanimously ratified** a law approving judicial cooperation between:

- **The Government of France**
- **The UN Independent Investigative Mechanism for Myanmar (IIMM)**

### Agreement Highlights
- Signed: **12 June 2024**, Geneva  
- Purpose: Collection, preservation, and sharing of evidence related to **grave international crimes**, including crimes against humanity.

## 2.2 Strategic Significance

This agreement:
- Strengthens **universal jurisdiction pathways**
- Enables future prosecutions beyond Myanmar’s borders
- Signals **long-term accountability**, not immediate justice

**Key Insight:**  
While militarily irrelevant in the short term, this development is **structurally threatening** to junta elites over time.

# 3. Junta Political Narrative: Elections as Continuity Mechanism

## 3.1 Official Statement

Senior General **Min Aung Hlaing** reiterated that:
> “After the election, state responsibilities will be transferred to the newly emerged government.”

This statement was delivered during visits to **Magway and Bago Regions** (21 December 2025).

## 3.2 Analytical Deconstruction

This narrative:
- Frames elections as **legitimacy theater**
- Avoids specifics on sovereignty, civilian control, or constitutional reform
- Mirrors previous **controlled transition rhetoric** (2010–2011)

**SSISM Assessment:**  
Elections are positioned as a **stability façade**, not a power transfer.

# 5. Information Warfare Layer: Military Lobbyists & “6800-Kyat Accounts”

## 5.1 Definition: Myanmar-Style Military Lobbyists

In contrast to international lobbying norms, Myanmar’s military lobbyists function as:

> **Non-uniformed propaganda auxiliaries**, tasked with narrative distortion, victim-blaming, and authoritarian normalization.

## 5.2 Taxonomy of Actors

### A. Senior Propaganda Figures
- Operate under real or semi-public identities
- Promote:
  - “Stability through military”
  - Economic collapse as “global issue”
  - Civilian suffering as unavoidable

### B. New-Generation “6800-Kyat” Accounts
Characteristics:
- Fake profiles
- Copy-paste messaging across groups
- Minimal political literacy
- High volume, low credibility output

|----|------------------|
