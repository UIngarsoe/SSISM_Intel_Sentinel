## 1️⃣ The Observable Fact

Since the coup, the UN has **not** replaced Myanmar’s Permanent Representative.

The individual appointed in October 2020 by the elected government remains in that position:

**Kyaw Moe Tun**

This is not symbolism.  
It is an institutional decision renewed every year by the UN Credentials process.

---

## 2️⃣ What the UN Credentials Process Means

The UN Credentials Committee does not make political statements.  
It performs a technical function:

> deciding which representative is accepted to speak for a member state.

By choosing **not** to accept a replacement nominee after the coup, the UN is signaling:

- This was **not** viewed as a normal constitutional transfer of power.
- The last uncontested diplomatic appointment remains valid in the UN system.

This is a form of **procedural non-recognition**.

## 3️⃣ Diplomatic Continuity vs Political Rupture

At the UN, none of these debates are spoken.

Instead, the system preserves the **last pre-rupture diplomatic fact**.

## 4️⃣ Why This Matters in International Law

In diplomatic practice, continuity often survives where politics collapses.

Embassies, treaties, and seats do not change hands automatically after coups.  
They change only when the international system accepts that a new authority is legitimate.

For Myanmar, that acceptance has **not** occurred at the UN level.
