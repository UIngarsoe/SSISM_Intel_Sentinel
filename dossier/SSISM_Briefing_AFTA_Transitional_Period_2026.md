## 1. Executive Summary: The Bridge to a New Nation

In Myanmar’s current political landscape, the 2008 Constitution — once the country’s operating system — has effectively been rendered non-functional by revolutionary realities on the ground. This has created a temporary vacuum in the legal and administrative structure of the state.

**AFTA (Arrangements for the Transitional Period)** is the proposed *interim bridge* designed to keep the country functioning from the moment the military junta falls until a permanent Federal Democratic Union is formally established.

This briefing explains what AFTA is, why progress appears slow, and what it means for ordinary citizens.

---

## 2. What is AFTA? (Simple Definition)

**AFTA stands for Arrangements for the Transitional Period.**

Think of it as a **temporary set of rules** that will govern Myanmar during the “gap year” between revolution and final constitutional settlement.

### The Problem
Writing a full constitution for a new federal country requires years of negotiation and trust-building.

### The Solution
AFTA acts as a **mini-constitution** that allows the transitional authorities (NUG and allied stakeholders) to:

- Manage the country
- Provide justice
- Maintain order
- Protect civilians

from day one of transition.

### The Three Pillars of AFTA

1. **Legitimacy** — Demonstrates to the world that the resistance is building, not only fighting.
2. **Safety** — Prevents chaos when the old system collapses.
3. **Inclusivity** — Defines fair power-sharing among ethnic groups and political actors.

## 4. Why Is There a “System Delay”?

Many observers ask why AFTA is not finalized quickly. In SSISM terms, this is **Institutionalized Delay** — a safety protocol, not inactivity.

### A. The Stakeholder Handshake

Just like computer systems require a handshake before data transfer, Myanmar’s key actors — NUG, EROs, NUCC, and political stakeholders — must all sign off on the same power-sharing code. Without this, the system risks crashing later.

### B. The Security Test

The military junta frequently attempts to create confusion through its own electoral narratives. A rushed AFTA could contain weaknesses exploitable by old-regime elements. Slow drafting is a **verification protocol**.

### C. The Value of “Not Rushing”

A rushed document could trigger internal disputes among dozens of armed and political groups. Delay allows for **verification and validation** of shared rules.
