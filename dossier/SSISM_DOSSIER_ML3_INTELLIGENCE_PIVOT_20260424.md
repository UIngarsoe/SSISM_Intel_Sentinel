## Executive Summary

On 23 April 2026, **Martial Law Order 3/2026 (ML3)** was signed not by the President, but by the newly appointed **Commander-in-Chief, Ye Win Oo**.

This is historically significant.

For the first time in modern Myanmar history, a former **Chief of Military Security Affairs (military intelligence chief)** is the Commander-in-Chief of the armed forces and the legal executor of nationwide martial law powers.

This creates a **dual structure of authority**:

- The **President** appears civilian and politically insulated.
- The **Commander-in-Chief** executes intelligence-driven repression under martial law.

This dossier explains why this structure is not accidental and what it means for civilians under ML3.

---

## 1. Who Signed ML3 — And Why That Matters

ML3 carries sweeping powers over:

- Civilian movement
- Communications
- Media activity
- Electronic devices
- Arrest and detention authority

The signature belongs to **Ye Win Oo**, Commander-in-Chief.

His background is not infantry command.  
His background is **military intelligence, surveillance, and internal security**.

ML3 is therefore not designed as a battlefield law.  
It is designed as a **population control and information control law**.

---

## 2. The Presidential Firewall

By transferring the Commander-in-Chief role to Ye Win Oo in March 2026, **Min Aung Hlaing** created a political buffer.

Under the 2008 Constitution:

- The Commander-in-Chief executes martial law.
- The President chairs the National Defence and Security Council (NDSC).
- The Commander-in-Chief still reports to the NDSC.

This means:

> Operational responsibility is pushed to the CiC,  
> while strategic authority remains with the President.

If future accountability questions arise over arrests, executions, or repression under ML3, the structure allows political blame to be redirected toward the military command.

This is a **structural shield**, not a transfer of power.

---

## 3. Why an Intelligence Chief as CiC Changes the Nature of Martial Law

Traditional martial law focuses on:

- Troop deployment
- Physical checkpoints
- Curfews

ML3, under an intelligence chief, emphasizes:

- Electronic communications (Sections 18/19)
- Device inspection
- Media monitoring
- Digital tracking
- Identification before detention

This transforms martial law into a **surveillance-led system**.

The objective is not only to control territory, but to **identify people through data**.

---

## 4. The Magway Visit: Psychological Framing

On the same day ML3 was issued, Ye Win Oo visited Magway and instructed troops to act with a “fatherly spirit.”

This messaging is important.

It provides moral reassurance to troops while legally enabling expanded powers over civilians.  
This combination of empathy rhetoric and expanded authority is a classic internal control method in intelligence-driven command structures.

## 5. What ML3 Means for Civilians

Under this structure, civilians are no longer exposed only to:

- Random searches
- Physical patrols

They are exposed to:

- Data-based identification
- Communication tracing
- Retroactive device checks
- Media activity monitoring
- Expanded arrest justification

The checkpoint is no longer only on the road.  
It is also in the phone.

---

## 6. Where Real Power Now Lies

The visible head of state is the President.

The operational executor of power is the Commander-in-Chief with an intelligence background.

The coordinating authority above both is the NDSC, chaired by the President.

This creates a system where:

> Intelligence identifies  
> Military executes  
> Presidency remains politically insulated
