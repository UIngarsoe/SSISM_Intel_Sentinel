# SSISM DOSSIER
## Briefing Paper No. 2026-04
**Date:** 19 April 2026  
**Title:** Chinese Influence in Northern Shan State and the Min Aung Hlaing Government’s Strategic Accommodation — Implications for Myanmar’s Stability and Sovereignty

---

## Executive Summary

Renewed fighting in March 2026 between the Myanmar National Democratic Alliance Army (MNDAA) and the Ta’ang National Liberation Army (TNLA) — including MNDAA’s capture of Kutkai and consolidation along the Muse–Namhkam corridor — highlights a decisive reality: northern Shan State is increasingly shaped by Beijing’s security priorities rather than Naypyidaw’s decisions.

China’s approach can be described as selective stabilisation focused on border security, trade continuity, and proxy management.

---

## 1. Background: The March 2026 Northern Shan Flashpoint

Northern Shan remains the epicentre of Myanmar’s fragmented civil war landscape since Operation 1027. The March 14 MNDAA offensive and seizure of Kutkai directly affected the Muse trade artery — Myanmar’s most vital commercial gateway to China.

---

## 2. Anatomy of Chinese Influence in Northern Shan

China’s leverage operates across proxy alignment, mediation with coercion, economic corridor protection, and divide‑and‑rule dynamics among EAOs.

---

## 3. The Government’s Strategic Accommodation

Signals include avoidance of direct intervention, acceptance of Chinese‑brokered realities, and elevation of China relations in cabinet structure.

---

## 4. Outlook

Short‑term stability may improve through Chinese support. Long‑term sovereignty erosion and conflict persistence remain likely.

---

## Conclusion

Northern Shan is being stabilised according to Beijing’s priorities, with Naypyidaw adapting rather than directing.
