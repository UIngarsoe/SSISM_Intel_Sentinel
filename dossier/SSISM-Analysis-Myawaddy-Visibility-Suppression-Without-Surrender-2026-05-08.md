# 🧭 Executive Summary

Following the **23 April 2026** directive issued by **Infantry Battalion 275 (IB-275)** in Myawaddy, the Tatmadaw initiated what appears to be an administrative consolidation effort intended to reduce visible armed presence across the border town.

The directive included:

- weapon deposit orders,
- uniform compliance rules,
- restrictions on armed personnel in public spaces,
- and tighter movement controls near strategic commercial zones.

However, the SSISM assessment concludes that this operation does **not** represent a genuine **DDR (Disarmament, Demobilization, Reintegration)** process.

Instead, the operation reflects:

# ⚠️ Visibility Suppression Without Structural Disarmament

Weapons are not being surrendered to centralized state control.

They are being:

- repositioned,
- concealed,
- internally reorganized,
- or retained within group-controlled facilities.

Meanwhile:

- armed loyalties,
- protection networks,
- local command structures,
- and economic extraction systems

remain substantially intact.
